From Halls to a Shared House Without the Stress

The jump from first-year halls to a second-year shared house is one of the most common student moves we handle across Sussex, and it rarely happens at a convenient moment. You are usually juggling exam results, a tenancy that ends on a fixed date, and a new landlord who wants you in on another fixed date — often with only a day or two of overlap. As a family-run removals team based at Doryln House in Ashington, we have spent years learning how to make these tight handovers run smoothly so you are never left carrying boxes between properties on foot. We will assess what you actually have — typically a bed’s worth of bedding, a few crates of kitchen equipment, books, a desk lamp and a wardrobe’s worth of clothes — and match it to the right size of vehicle rather than overselling you a full removal lorry you simply do not need. For most halls-to-house moves a single trip is all it takes, and our man and van service in Sussex is usually the perfect fit. If your new shared house is unfurnished and you have picked up a sofa or a chest of drawers from family, we will factor that in and bring the right team and equipment to lift it safely up the stairs.

Moving House Between Terms & Returning Home for Summer

University life runs on a calendar that no other type of move follows, and we plan around it. Two pinch points dominate the academic year: the end of the summer term, when contracts expire and thousands of students need to clear their rooms within the same fortnight, and the start of the autumn term, when everyone wants to be in and settled before lectures begin. Because demand spikes so sharply in these windows, the single most useful thing you can do is book early — ideally as soon as your tenancy dates are confirmed. When you ask us for a free student removals quote we will pencil in your preferred slot and confirm exactly what we are moving, so there are no surprises on the day. We also understand that summer creates a particular puzzle: your term-time tenancy ends in June or July, but your next one may not start until September. Rather than hauling everything back to a family home two hours away and then bringing it all back again, many students leave the bulk of their belongings with us and simply take home what they need for the holidays. We can collect from your Sussex house share, transport your goods, and either deliver them straight to your new address in the autumn or hold them in between.

Why End-of-Tenancy Timing Matters So Much

Student tenancies are notoriously unforgiving about dates. Your agreement will specify a precise check-out time, your deposit depends on leaving the property clean and empty, and the incoming tenants are frequently arriving the very same day. Getting the timing of your removal right is therefore not just about convenience — it directly protects your deposit. We build our schedules around these realities and will agree a collection window that leaves you enough time to do a final clean and hand back the keys without panic. A few practical points we always raise with students at the booking stage:

  • Confirm your exact check-out date and time before you book, then give us the same window so we arrive with room to spare.
  • Strip and dismantle anything the landlord supplied only if your contract requires it — otherwise leave fixtures in place to avoid disputes.
  • Photograph the empty room once it is cleared, as proof of condition for your deposit return.
  • Don’t leave bin bags, broken furniture or unwanted items behind — tell us in advance and we can advise on responsible disposal rather than risk a deduction.
  • If several housemates are leaving on the same day, group your collections so the whole property is cleared in one organised visit.

Because we are a fully insured Sussex removals company carrying £10m liability cover, your belongings are protected from the moment we load them, which matters when you are moving the laptop and notes your whole degree depends on.

Small, Affordable Loads — Pay for What You Actually Move

One of the biggest worries we hear from students is cost, and it is an entirely fair one. A typical student move is small compared with a family home, so it makes no sense to be charged as though you are relocating a four-bedroom house. The way we keep student moves affordable is simple: we size the job honestly. If you have a single room’s worth of belongings, we send a single van and a small, efficient crew rather than a fleet. If you and two housemates are moving on the same day to addresses near each other, we can sometimes combine collections to save everyone money. We never pad a quote with services you do not need, and we will always tell you when a lighter option will do the job just as well. Take a look at how we structure our transparent removals pricing so you understand exactly what is included before you commit. For genuinely compact loads — a few crates, a duvet, a desk chair and a couple of suitcases — our flexible man and van option is almost always the most economical route, giving you a driver and a helper with a properly equipped vehicle for as long as the job takes and no more.

International Students & Flexible Scheduling Around Term Dates

International students face an extra layer of complexity because the journey home is rarely an option for the holidays. If you are flying back to your home country for the summer, you cannot reasonably take a year’s worth of belongings with you, and shipping it twice would be both costly and impractical. This is where our combined moving and storage approach is most valuable: we collect from your Sussex accommodation before you leave, hold everything securely while you are away, and have it ready in your new room the moment you return. Because we cover the UK and Europe as well as the whole of Sussex, Surrey, Hampshire and Kent, we can also help if your studies are taking you to a new city rather than back to halls. Throughout, we schedule entirely around the academic calendar — collection dates that fit your check-out, delivery dates that fit the start of term, and the flexibility to adjust if your results or visa arrangements shift your plans at short notice. The simplest first step is to call us on 01903 893731 or email contact@wolves-removals.co.uk and tell us your term dates; we will build the rest of the plan around them.

Combining Your Move With Student Storage

Storage and student life go hand in hand, and combining the two in a single arrangement is where we save students the most time and money. The classic scenario is the summer gap: you must vacate one property in July but cannot move into the next until September. Rather than two long journeys home and back, we collect everything in one visit, keep it secure with us over the break, and deliver it to your new address ready for the autumn term. Our dedicated student storage service is designed precisely for these academic-year gaps, while our short-term storage options suit shorter overlaps of just a few weeks between tenancies or while a new house is being decorated. Storing with the same company that moves you removes a whole layer of hassle — there is no separate handling fee, no second firm to coordinate, and your belongings stay in one chain of custody from the moment we collect to the moment we deliver. As a LAPADA member and Checkatrade-verified team recommended by agents including Fine & Country, Justin Lloyd and Mansell McTaggart, we apply the same care to a student’s storage as we would to a family’s, so your things come back exactly as they went in.

What to Pack — A Student’s Practical Checklist

Good packing is what turns a chaotic student move into a calm one, and after years of helping undergraduates and postgraduates across West Sussex, East Sussex, Surrey, Hampshire and Kent we have seen exactly what goes wrong when it is rushed. Sturdy boxes beat bin bags every time: bags tear on staircases, hide what is inside, and offer no protection for anything breakable. We supply proper cartons, tape, bubble wrap and marker pens through our range of packing materials and boxes, so you can pack methodically over a week rather than in a single frantic night. A few habits make a real difference:

  • Pack heavy items such as books into small boxes and lighter items such as bedding into large ones, so nothing becomes too heavy to carry.
  • Label every box with the room it belongs in and a one-line note of the contents — ‘kitchen: mugs, plates’ saves you opening five boxes to find breakfast.
  • Keep a clearly marked ‘first night’ box with your charger, toothbrush, a change of clothes, a towel and your course essentials.
  • Back up your laptop and carry your documents, passport and any tenancy paperwork with you personally rather than in the van.
  • Use clothes, towels and bedding as free padding around fragile kitchen items to cut down on bubble wrap.

If you would rather hand the whole thing over, our team can pack for you as part of a wider house removals service scaled to a student budget — particularly useful in exam season when your time is better spent revising than wrapping glasses.
