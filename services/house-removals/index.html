Moving Day Hour By Hour: What Actually Happens

One of the most common questions we are asked is simply, ‘What will the day feel like?’ Knowing the rhythm of a move in advance takes a great deal of the anxiety out of it. Our crews arrive promptly, usually first thing, and your dedicated move coordinator confirms the plan with you before any furniture is touched. The team then lays protection through the property, works methodically room by room loading the van in a deliberate order, and keeps you informed at each stage. Once the property is clear, we do a final walk-through together so nothing is left behind, then travel to your new home where the process runs in reverse — furniture placed where you want it, boxes set down in the correct rooms, and beds reassembled so you have somewhere to sleep that night.

A typical move tends to follow this shape:

  • Arrival & brief: introductions, a quick walk-round, and agreement on which items are priorities and which stay behind.
  • Protection & preparation: floor runners, banister and corner padding, and door guards go in before anything is carried.
  • Loading: heavy and base items first, fragile and awkward pieces wrapped and braced, everything logged room by room.
  • Transit & unload: a steady journey to your new address, then a careful unload with each box placed in its labelled room.
  • Setting up: beds and key furniture reassembled, a final tidy of packing materials, and a last check before we leave.

Because every job has a single point of contact, there is always one person who knows the whole picture — useful if a question comes up mid-move or plans shift on the day.

Protecting Your Floors, Furniture & Doorways

Most accidental damage during a move happens not to the items being carried but to the home itself — scuffed skirting, marked walls, and scratched floors. We treat both properties as if they were our own. Before loading begins we lay protective runners across carpets and hard floors along the main carrying routes, pad banisters and tight corners, and fit guards to door frames at risk of knocks. Sofas, mattresses and upholstered chairs are wrapped to keep them clean, while polished and antique furniture is blanket-wrapped and, where needed, given an extra layer of protection at vulnerable edges and feet.

Our care for delicate and high-value pieces is one reason we hold LAPADA membership and are Checkatrade-verified — the handling standards expected of fine-art and antiques transport carry over into every ordinary family move we do. For total reassurance we are fully insured with £10m liability cover, and our work is recommended by respected local estate agents including Fine & Country, Justin Lloyd and Mansell McTaggart. Glassware, mirrors, artwork and electronics each get wrapping appropriate to the item rather than a one-size-fits-all approach, and anything genuinely irreplaceable is flagged at the survey so we can plan its handling in advance.

Dismantling, Reassembly & The Optional Extras That Help

Large family homes are full of furniture that simply will not fit through a doorway or down a staircase in one piece. Our crews are equipped and experienced to dismantle and reassemble wardrobes, bed frames, bunk beds, flat-pack units, garden furniture, trampolines and dining tables as part of the move, keeping fixings safely bagged and labelled so everything goes back together correctly at the other end. It is the kind of detail that turns a daunting day into a manageable one.

If completion dates do not line up, our secure household storage solutions bridge the gap, and you can size up what you need with our handy online storage calculator. For smaller jobs, single items or a part-load, our flexible man and van service across Sussex may be all you need, while businesses relocating premises are covered by our commercial removals team.

Moving With Children Or Pets & Choosing The Right Date

Families rarely move in isolation — there are children, pets, school terms and work commitments all in the mix. Our advice, built up over years of moving households across West Sussex and the surrounding counties, is to plan around the people and animals who feel the upheaval most. Where possible, arrange for young children and pets to spend moving day with a relative, friend or in a quiet room away from the front door; it keeps them calm and keeps our carrying routes clear and safe. Pack an essentials box — chargers, kettle, snacks, medication, a change of clothes, favourite toys and pet bowls — and keep it with you rather than on the van, so the first evening in your new home is comfortable from the start.

Choosing the right date matters too. Mid-week and mid-month moves are often quieter and more flexible than the Friday and end-of-month rush, which can make scheduling and parking easier at both ends. We will always talk through the realities of your chain and completion timings honestly so you book a date that genuinely works.
