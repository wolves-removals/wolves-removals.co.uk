Five Reasons to Move to West Sussex

Considering a move to West Sussex? Here are five reasons it’s one of the most desirable places to live in the South East.

West Sussex is one of England’s most appealing counties — a blend of beautiful coast, rolling countryside, historic towns and easy access to London. As a Sussex removals company based near Pulborough, we help people move here all the time. Here are five reasons West Sussex might be your next home.

1. Stunning Coast and Countryside

From the beaches of Worthing, Bognor Regis and Littlehampton to the rolling hills of the South Downs National Park, West Sussex offers an enviable natural setting. Whether you love the sea or the countryside, it’s all within easy reach.

2. Great Transport Links

West Sussex is brilliantly connected. Direct trains reach London in around an hour from towns like Horsham and Haywards Heath, Gatwick Airport is on the doorstep, and the A23/M23 and A27 make getting around straightforward — perfect for commuters and travellers alike.

3. Characterful Towns and Villages

From the cathedral city of Chichester to historic Arundel with its castle, and bustling market towns like Horsham, West Sussex is full of character. There’s a real mix of vibrant centres and peaceful villages to suit every lifestyle.

4. Culture, History and Events

West Sussex is rich in culture — Chichester Festival Theatre, Goodwood’s racing and motorsport events, Arundel Castle, and festivals throughout the year. There’s always something on, and the heritage runs deep.

5. A Wonderful Place to Raise a Family

With excellent schools, green space, a strong sense of community and a generally relaxed pace of life, West Sussex is a fantastic place to put down roots. It’s no surprise so many families choose to move here.

Thinking of making the move? We know the county inside out and would love to help you settle in. Explore the areas we cover in West Sussex or request a free quote.

Wide Open Spaces and the South Downs on Your Doorstep

One of the quiet joys of life in West Sussex is just how easy it is to step out of your front door and find yourself surrounded by rolling countryside. The chalk hills of the South Downs sweep across the county, offering miles of footpaths, bridleways and gentle ridge walks where the views open out across patchwork fields towards the sea. Families who move here often say that the sheer amount of green space is what wins them over — you can spend a weekend exploring without ever feeling like you have run out of fresh routes to try.

Whether you fancy a long ramble across the Downs, a gentle stroll through ancient woodland or a steady cycle along quiet country lanes, the landscape rewards every kind of pace. The seasons bring their own character too: bluebells carpeting the woods in spring, warm golden light over the hills in late summer, and crisp, clear mornings in autumn when the air smells of woodsmoke and damp leaves. If outdoor life matters to you, settling into one of the villages or towns we serve across West Sussex removals puts all of this within easy reach, and that sense of space is something most newcomers grow to treasure.

The Coast, the Seaside Towns and a Slower Pace by the Water

West Sussex is blessed with a generous stretch of coastline, and that proximity to the sea shapes daily life in the most pleasant ways. The seaside towns along the coast each have their own personality — some bustling and lively with promenades and independent cafes, others quieter and more reflective, where the loudest sound is the shingle shifting under the tide. Wherever you choose to put down roots, you are rarely more than a short journey from a beach walk, a paddle or simply a flask of tea watching the waves roll in.

Coastal living suits people who like a slower, more grounded rhythm. There is something restorative about being near the water, and many of those relocating from busier cities find the change of pace does them the world of good. If you are weighing up a move towards the shore, our guide to 10 things to know about moving to Worthing gives a friendly sense of what coastal life in the county can feel like, while our wider helpful tips cover plenty of other corners worth getting to know before you commit.

Food, Drink, Farm Shops and Local Markets

If you appreciate good food and the simple pleasure of knowing where it comes from, West Sussex will feel like home very quickly. The county has a strong farming heritage, and that translates into a wonderful network of farm shops, roadside stalls and seasonal markets where you can pick up fresh produce, local cheeses, baked goods and other treats. Shopping this way connects you to the rhythm of the seasons and to the people who grow and make what ends up on your table — a small thing, perhaps, but one that makes a place feel like more than just an address.

Beyond the markets, the county is dotted with characterful country pubs, welcoming tea rooms and independent eateries, many of them tucked into pretty villages where you would never expect to find them. Some of the gentle pleasures newcomers grow fond of include:

  • Browsing weekend markets for seasonal fruit, vegetables and locally made goods
  • Discovering tucked-away country pubs with proper home-cooked food
  • Picking up fresh bread and treats from independent bakeries
  • Visiting farm shops to support local growers and producers

Part of the fun of relocating is exploring all of this for yourself, and there is no quicker way to feel settled than finding your own favourite spot for a Sunday lunch or a morning coffee.

Getting Around West Sussex

For all its rural charm, West Sussex is a surprisingly well-connected place to live. The county sits within comfortable reach of the wider South East, so whether you are commuting, visiting family or simply heading off to explore, you will find that getting around is generally straightforward. A network of main roads links the towns and villages, and the coast, the Downs and the neighbouring counties are all within easy driving distance for a day out.

Public transport offers another option for those who would rather leave the car at home, with services connecting many of the larger towns. For longer journeys, the broader region provides good links towards London and the rest of the country, which is part of what makes the area so appealing to people who want countryside living without feeling cut off. When the time comes to actually make your move, our team can travel throughout the county and beyond — we regularly cover the areas we serve across Sussex, Surrey, Hampshire and Kent, so wherever you are heading, we can usually get you there.

A General Guide to the Different Parts of West Sussex

One of the lovely things about West Sussex is its variety. The county packs several distinct kinds of place into a relatively compact area, and getting a feel for these broad differences can help you decide where you might be happiest. Nothing here is set in stone — every town and village has its own flavour — but in general terms, you will find a few recognisable types of location.

Along the coast you have the seaside towns, where life revolves around the shore, the promenades and that breezy, open feeling that comes with living near the water. Inland, around the Downs, the mood shifts to one of rolling countryside, quiet lanes and villages that feel pleasantly tucked away among the hills. The market towns sit somewhere in between, offering characterful high streets, independent shops and a strong sense of community while still being surrounded by greenery.

  • Coastal areas — sea air, beach walks and a lively, open atmosphere
  • The Downs — rolling hills, peaceful villages and easy access to walking country
  • Market towns — characterful high streets, local shops and a friendly community feel

If you are still narrowing down where to settle, it is always worth visiting at different times of the week to get a true sense of a place. Our locations pages give a broad overview of the many areas we cover, which can be a handy starting point as you build your shortlist.

Settling Into Your New Area

Moving to a new part of the country is exciting, but it can also feel a little daunting in the first few weeks. The good news is that West Sussex tends to be a warm and welcoming place, and there are plenty of small steps you can take to feel at home more quickly. The simplest advice is to get out and explore — walk your new neighbourhood, find the nearest shop, work out your favourite route to the park or the beach, and say hello to the people you meet along the way.

Joining in with local life is another reliable way to put down roots. Community groups, local events and clubs are a natural way to meet people who share your interests, and you will often find that one introduction leads to another. Give yourself permission to take your time, too: it is perfectly normal for a new place to feel unfamiliar at first, and most people find that within a season or two it starts to feel properly like home.

  • Take regular walks to learn the layout of your new neighbourhood
  • Find your nearest essentials — the shop, the post box, the doctor
  • Look out for community groups and local events to meet new faces
  • Be patient with yourself; settling in takes a little time

A smooth move is the best possible start, which is why so many people arriving in the county choose to begin their new chapter with a calm, well-organised moving day rather than a stressful one.
